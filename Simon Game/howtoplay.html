How to Play Simon Game

1. Game Setup: The Simon game features a series of colored buttons, typically arranged in a circular pattern. There's also a central console that controls the game.
2. Players: There is one player in the Simon game. The game itself acts as the opposing player, displaying sequences of colors and sounds that the player must remember and repeat.
3. Gameplay: The game begins with Simon displaying a sequence of colors or sounds using the colored buttons. The player's task is to mimic the sequence by pressing the buttons in the same order.
4. Memory Challenge: Each successful repetition of the sequence results in Simon adding one more step to the pattern. The player must remember the entire sequence correctly to continue.
5. Winning Conditions: The player wins by correctly repeating the increasingly complex sequence set by Simon. The game ends if the player successfully repeats a long enough sequence or if they make a mistake.
6. Mistakes: If the player presses the wrong button or fails to repeat the sequence accurately, Simon signals the mistake, and the game may end or give the player another chance to try.
7. Game Over: The game is over when the player either successfully completes a very long sequence or makes a mistake. To play again, simply restart the game and challenge yourself to beat your previous score.
Enjoy the challenge of Simon, where memory and concentration are your greatest assets!
